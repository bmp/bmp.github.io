2. Longer version

Here is the longer version of the bio:

Bharath is a researcher and designer of tools to enhance public policy through the lens of complex adaptive systems. His work spans various critical areas such as inequality, transport, energy, disaster management, urban planning, governance, and social protection. With numerous academic publications to his name, his work has also been featured in the media.

In 2012, Bharath co-founded Fields of View, a non-profit research organization in India dedicated to creating tools for collaborative public policy. His contributions to public policy development earned him election to the Ashoka Fellowship in 2018. At Fields of View, he pioneered tools and methods that allow stakeholders to collaboratively design and implement public policy solutions. Bharath is co-founding Tarkam to improve data-driven discourse globally.

He occasionally teaches specialized courses in Tools for Participation in Public Policy, Games as Research Tools, and Data in Public Policy. Beyond his professional work, Bharath is co-founding a new organization, Tarkam, aimed at enhancing data-driven discourse across the world.

In addition to his academic and policy-related endeavors, Bharath curates a list of books on Bangalore and enjoys sending postcards with poems that resonate with contemporary issues. His personal interests also include collecting handmade fountain pens, practicing Esperanto, and exploring the nuances of tea as a certified tea taster. Since 2009, he has maintained an online presence and recently adopted KeyOxide as his identity management system.
